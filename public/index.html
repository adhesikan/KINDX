Project Description

KINDX utility and participation overview

KINDX is a community-driven utility token on the Base blockchain designed to enable tipping, participation, and positive-impact activities within its ecosystem. The token is used to support community engagement, reward contributors, and facilitate on-chain interactions related to giving and social participation. KINDX is not an investment product and does not provide any rights to profits, dividends, or governance over any entity.

A crypto movement for generosity

How to Get KINDX

Users may acquire KINDX through decentralized exchanges, community participation programs, or community distributions. These methods provide access to the token for use within the KINDX ecosystem and do not represent an investment opportunity.

Access via decentralized exchange

Swap ETH for KINDX on a decentralized exchange such as Uniswap on the Base blockchain. KINDX is used for community participation, tipping, and engagement and is not an investment product.

Community participation programs

Earn KINDX by participating in community programs such as tipping, donating, or contributing to positive-impact activities that are recognized by the KINDX community.

Community distributions

Receive KINDX through community airdrops or reward distributions to early supporters, creators, and participants. There is no promise of profit, return, or appreciation.

Acquisition and Distribution

Community members access KINDX in three compliant ways designed for utility and participation, not financial returns.

  • Decentralized exchange access: some tokens move through on-chain swaps on DEXs like Uniswap for people who want to participate on Base.
  • Community participation programs: allocations support the Be Kind, Earn KINDX initiative for tipping, donating, and verified good deeds.
  • Airdrops and community distributions: portions are set aside for airdrops to early supporters, creators, and impact partners.

For Data Providers: KINDX is a decentralized token with no issuer, no profit-sharing mechanism, and no centralized entity providing financial returns. Data providers such as CoinGecko and CoinMarketCap list KINDX for informational tracking purposes only.
